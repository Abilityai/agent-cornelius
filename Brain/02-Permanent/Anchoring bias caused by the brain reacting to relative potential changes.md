[[Brain reacts to relative potential change, not to absolute change]]

The anchoring bias occurs when a person relies too heavily on a single piece of information, or "anchor," when making a decision. This anchor can be a numerical value, such as a price or a weight, or it can be a non-numerical value, such as a person's appearance or a product's brand name.
