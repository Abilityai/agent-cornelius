## The Loop

A circular neurological-cognitive reinforcement system:

1. **Uncertainty triggers dopamine** (exploration/curiosity drive)
   - [[Uncertainty in itself is rewarding can create Dopamine spikes]]
   - [[Uncertain Cue gives a higher Dopamine reward than certain one]]

2. **Beliefs resolve uncertainty** (temporary dopamine spike from resolution)
   - [[Belief is a way to deal with Uncertainty, to explain parts of the world that we can't explain]]
   - [[Human brain looks for certainty and doesn't like uncertainty]]

3. **Confirmation of beliefs triggers additional dopamine** (reward reinforcement)
   - [[Finding a confirmation of the belief creates a spike of Dopamine]]
   - [[Confirmation Bias]] creates neurological reward

4. **Challenge to beliefs creates uncertainty** â†’ return to step 1
   - [[Once we hold a Belief, we will face people who have opposite beliefs]]
   - Creates cognitive dissonance and uncertainty

## Why Changing Beliefs Is Neurologically Difficult

Belief change requires:
- **Accepting uncertainty** (dopamine withdrawal from loss of certainty)
- **Giving up confirmation rewards** (ongoing dopamine hits from validation)
- **Facing contradictory evidence** (creates aversive uncertainty state)

**Double neurological cost:** Both withdrawal AND loss of reward stream.

## Implications

- Different relationship with dopamine: reward from navigation, not resolution
- Manages belief without needing certainty
