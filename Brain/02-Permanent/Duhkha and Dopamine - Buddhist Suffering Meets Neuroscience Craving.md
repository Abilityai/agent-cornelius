## The Recursive Craving Problem

### Buddhist Insight
From [[Buddhism plain and simple - Steve Hagen]]:
> "When we want something, e.g. pizza, we don't just notice that we want it, but we want to stop wanting pizza, which creates more craving itself"

**Structure:**
- Primary craving: "I want X"
- Secondary craving: "I want to not want X" (discomfort with desire itself)
- Tertiary craving: "I want to be free from wanting" (spiritual materialism)
- Infinite regress of wanting

### Dopamine Parallel
1. **Primary dopamine:** Wanting the thing
2. **Secondary dopamine:** Wanting relief from wanting (discomfort of craving)
3. **Compensatory mechanisms:** After peak, drop below baseline creates aversive state
4. **Next seeking:** Dopamine spike to escape the drop
5. **Addiction:** When baseline so low that craving never stops

[[Dopamine]] note: "This is where the addiction comes from - when the peaks are so high, and baseline is so low, that without an activity or a drug it is impossible to motivate oneself."
