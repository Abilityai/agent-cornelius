**Core Insight** (in author's voice):

Once we explain something - this something loses a magic, it stops being interesting.

---

**Context & Reasoning**:

This observation identifies a **fundamental tension between understanding and wonder**. The act of explaining something removes its mysterious quality, and with that mystery goes the interest.

**The Paradox**:
- Curiosity drives us to explain
- Explanation satisfies curiosity
- Satisfaction kills curiosity
- Result: We lose interest in what we understand

**Why Explanation Destroys Magic**:

1. **Mystery = Uncertain possibility**
   - Unexplained things contain infinite potential
   - Explanation collapses possibilities to one reality

2. **Interest = Uncertainty-driven exploration**
   - We're interested when outcome is uncertain
   - [[Uncertain Cue gives a higher Dopamine reward than certain one]] (likely exists in vault)
   - Once explained, no uncertainty remains → no dopamine → no interest

3. **Magic = Unexplained wonder**
   - Magic requires not-knowing
   - Explanation = Reduction to mechanism
   - Mechanism lacks wonder

**Implications**:

**For Learning**:
- Too much explanation too soon kills natural curiosity
- Optimal learning preserves some mystery
- Teaching should maintain zones of wonder

**For Creativity**:
- Artists deliberately avoid full explanation
- Great art resists complete understanding
- Over-analysis destroys creative appreciation

**For Science**:
- Scientific progress = Continuous de-magification
- Each explanation creates new mysteries at deeper levels
- The universe stays interesting by revealing new unknowns

**For Buddhism**:
- Buddhist teaching: Explanation removes direct experience
- Conceptual understanding ≠ Experiential knowing
- Enlightenment requires releasing explanatory frameworks

**The Trade-off**:
- **Gain**: Predictability, control, practical utility
- **Loss**: Wonder, mystery, aesthetic appreciation

**Personal Application**:
Should we sometimes resist explaining things we find magical? Is there value in deliberate not-knowing?
