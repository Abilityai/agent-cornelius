**Title**: [[Samsara as Infinite Regress - The Bottomless Well of Conceptual Description]]
**Uniqueness**: Reframes samsara not just as suffering from desire, but as an **epistemological trap** - the infinite regress of describing feelings with feelings. **Core Insight** (in author's voice):

We describe our feelings using terms of already recognized feelings. It seems we're getting deeper understanding - like descending into a well with different levels that widens as it deepens (learning more and more details). But this flow of feelings turning into descriptions has no end. **This well has no bottom. There is no final description, and the eternal search for this final description is samsara itself.**

---

**Context & Reasoning**:

The traditional Buddhist understanding of samsara focuses on suffering through craving and attachment. This insight adds a critical epistemological dimension: **samsara is the trap of seeking complete understanding through conceptualization**.

**The Infinite Regress Mechanism:**
1. We experience a feeling
2. We describe it using previously recognized feelings/concepts
3. This creates the illusion of understanding
4. But each description requires further description
5. The well gets wider (more complexity) as we go deeper
6. There is no bottom - no final, complete description

**The Paradox:**
- More detailed conceptual knowledge ≠ deeper understanding
- Seeking clarity through concepts generates infinite complexity
- The search for the "final description" IS the suffering (samsara)
- Direct experience requires abandoning the search, not completing it

**Why this matters:**
- Explains why intellectual understanding alone doesn't end suffering
- Shows why meditation/direct perception is necessary (stops the regress)
- Reveals conceptualization as self-perpetuating (each answer creates new questions)
- Links Buddhist philosophy to epistemological limits (Gödel-like incompleteness)
