"One of the crucial differences between a strong leader and an ordinary one is the kind of people they surround themselves with. Every book on decision-making, every book on leadership, and every management consultant will tell you the same thing: If you surround yourself with agreeable Yes-men - you are going to lose."

---

**Context & Reasoning**:

Eugene explains four specific mechanisms by which yes-men environments destroy decision quality:

**1. Echo Chamber Amplification**
"Confirmatory groups create echo chambers of opinions that greatly amplify the effects of the Confirmation and Desirability biases. Basically, if you've agreeable people around you, you'll always be more convinced of your worldview and not question or check it often enough."

**2. Overconfidence Escalation**
"These groups will cause you to suffer Overconfidence bias, which makes you stick to your original plans instead of changing course in time."

**3. Blind Spot Perpetuation**
"You won't notice your own biases, and others won't point them out to you. It's difficult for many people to recognize and get rid of their cognitive biases, but easy for them to recognize them in the judgments of others. Basically, we need others with a wildcard to disagree with us to make better decisions."

**4. Opinion Suppression**
"In Yes-man groups, you won't take the insightful opinions of others over the noise of your own genius. People will just agree with you and give up their critically evaluated thoughts."

---

**The Solution: Constructive Conflict**

Eugene distinguishes critical types of conflict:
- **NOT personal conflict** directed against personality
- **YES "task conflict"** over ideas and opinions
- This triggers: creativity, innovation, better decision-making

**How to Create Non-Confirmatory Environment**:

**Hire Right People:**
"Surround yourself with people who're looking for the best answer, rather than feeling they're right; people who're willing to challenge your opinion, rather than yes-men who want to make you happy."

**Create Psychological Safety:**
"Create a safe space where you can learn something new when you're wrong, not when you're being punitive."

**Maintain Focus on Ideas:**
"Make sure people don't get caught up in personal conflicts, but instead practice constructive engagement with things (ideas, opinions)"

**Encourage Challenge:**
"Encourage your team to challenge your thoughts and ideas and to catch yourself and others when you fall victim to various biases."
