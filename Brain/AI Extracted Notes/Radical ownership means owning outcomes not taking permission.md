## Context

Eugene contrasts technical delegation with true ownership through personal experience: hiring smart people who were "technically brilliant, great on paper" but everything still came back to him with questions like "What's the final call?" This looked like delegation but wasn't ownership.

**Key Insight:** "I don't want people asking for permission. I want them owning the outcomes. Not in a checkbox, task-oriented way, but in the way a founder treats a company."

In the AI age, this becomes even more critical: "AI can follow instructions faster and cheaper than any team. But it can't own an outcome. It can't carry conviction. It can't see the blind spots, shift strategy, or lead from the front."

The question Eugene asks: "Are you building a team of list-checkers? Or a team that owns all of their work?"
