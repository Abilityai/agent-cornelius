**Core Insight** (in author's voice):

We describe our feelings using terms of already recognized feelings, creating the illusion that we better understand the world and ourselves. We imagine ourselves in a well with different levels—the deeper we go, the wider it becomes (learning more and more details). But in reality, this flow of feelings transforming into concepts has no end. The well has no bottom. There is no final description, and the eternal search for this final description **is samsara itself**.

---

**Context & Reasoning**:

**The Infinite Regress Mechanism:**
- Feelings described by → already-categorized feelings → which were themselves described by earlier feelings → ad infinitum
- Each layer of description creates new distinctions requiring further description
- The well "widens as it deepens"—more detail generates more complexity, not resolution
- No final ontology exists; the pursuit of complete description is the trap

**Samsara Reframed:**
Traditional Buddhism presents samsara as the cycle of suffering through attachment and desire. This insight reframes it specifically as an **epistemological trap**—the addictive pursuit of complete understanding through ever-finer conceptual distinctions.

**The Illusion of Progress:**
- Moving "deeper" feels like enlightenment (more refined categories)
- Actually generates more surface area requiring explanation
- Mistake: confusing increased detail with increased understanding
- Reality: Categories proliferate without reaching bedrock truth

**Why It's Endless:**
- Feelings are qualia—direct experience
- Language/concepts are categorical—maps not territory
- Describing qualia with categories = fundamental type mismatch
- Each categorization layer adds distance from direct experience
- More categories → more gaps → more need for categories
