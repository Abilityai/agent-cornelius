**Core Insight** (in author's voice):

Я выбираю путь в котором я могу больше всего предсказать. Когда решаю как идти, то есть совершаю любой выбор - я считаю рациональным выбирать то, где я вижу больше определенности. Я просто делаю выбор в сторону более предсказуемых ситуаций, а другие могут по другому наверное. Это для меня такой путь рациональности.

Страх это когда мы в выборе дальнейших действий опираемся на оценивание ситуаций с точки зрения максимизиции определенности.

[Translation: I choose the path where I can predict the most. When deciding how to go, that is, making any choice - I consider it rational to choose where I see more certainty. I simply make choices toward more predictable situations, others might do differently. This is my path of rationality.

Fear is when we, in choosing further actions, rely on evaluating situations from the point of view of maximizing certainty.]

**Context & Reasoning**:

This is a **personal definition of rationality** that equates rational choice with choosing maximum predictability. It then makes a striking connection: **fear operates by the same mechanism**.

**The Framework**:

**Personal Rationality = Certainty Maximization**
- Rational choice = Choose the most predictable option
- More certainty = Better choice
- Less uncertainty = More rational

**Fear = Certainty Optimization**
- Fear-based decisions = Maximize certainty/predictability
- Avoid uncertainty = Avoid fear
- Therefore: Fear is "rational" by this definition

**The Disturbing Implication**:

If:
- Rationality = Choosing maximum predictability
- Fear = Choosing maximum certainty

Then: **Rationality = Fear-driven decision making**

**Is rationality just fear dressed up as logic?**

**Alternative Rationality Frameworks**:

The author acknowledges "others might do differently" - suggesting awareness that this is ONE definition, not THE definition:

**Other Possible Rationalities**:
1. **Expected value maximization** - Choose highest expected reward (standard economics)
2. **Exploration-exploitation balance** - Sometimes choose uncertain options to learn
3. **Regret minimization** - Choose to avoid future regret
4. **Satisficing** - Choose "good enough" rather than optimal
5. **Courage-based** - Choose the meaningful/valuable even if uncertain
