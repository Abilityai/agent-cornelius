## Core Insight

Information-theoretic frameworks (specifically partial information decomposition of time-delayed mutual information) provide rigorous mathematical tools to distinguish genuine emergent coordination from simple aggregation in multi-agent systems, enabling the transition from "mere aggregates to higher-order collectives" to be objectively measured and systematically engineered.

---

## Context & Evidence

**Mathematical Framework:**
- **Partial Information Decomposition (PID)**: Separates shared information into unique, redundant, and synergistic components
- **Time-Delayed Mutual Information**: Captures temporal coordination patterns
- **Synergy Measurement**: Quantifies when collective behavior exceeds sum of individual contributions

**Experimental Validation:**

1. **Control Condition**
   - Showed temporal synergy with minimal cross-agent alignment
   - Agents operating in parallel, not coordination

2. **Persona Assignment**
   - Introduced stable identity-linked differentiation
   - Measurable increase in complementary information

3. **Personas + Collaborative Instruction**
   - Goal-directed complementarity
   - Information-theoretic signature of genuine collective intelligence

**Significance:**
- Moves field from qualitative claims ("agents work together") to quantitative measurement
- Distinguishes spurious coupling from performance-relevant synergy
- Enables systematic optimization of coordination mechanisms
