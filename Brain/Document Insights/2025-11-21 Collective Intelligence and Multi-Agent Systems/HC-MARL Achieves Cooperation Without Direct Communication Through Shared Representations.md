## Core Insight

Hierarchical Consensus-based Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (HC-MARL) employs contrastive learning to foster global consensus, enabling cooperative behavior without direct communication between agents. Agents coordinate through shared representations learned through contrastive learning, creating implicit coordination that bypasses message-passing overhead.

---

## Context & Evidence

**Mechanism**: Contrastive learning aligns agent representations in shared latent space. Agents that experience similar states develop similar internal representations, enabling coordinated behavior without explicit message exchange.

**Advantage**: Eliminates communication overhead entirely while maintaining coordination quality. Agents "understand" each other through aligned world models rather than verbal exchange.

**Analogy**: Similar to how humans coordinate through shared culture and mental models rather than constantly verbalizing intentions. Implicit understanding through aligned representations.
