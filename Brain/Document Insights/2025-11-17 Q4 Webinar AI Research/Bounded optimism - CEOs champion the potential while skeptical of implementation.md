# Bounded optimism - CEOs champion the potential while skeptical of implementation

## Core Insight

The 2025 CEO mindset is "Bounded Optimism" - they are champions of AI's potential (61% actively adopting AI agents, expecting investment growth to double) while simultaneously being deep skeptics of implementation (aware of 95% failure rate, disconnected technology, and AI trust gap). They champion the goal while being deeply skeptical of how their organization will get there.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The dual mindset:**

**Champions of the potential:**
- **61%** actively adopting AI agents (IBM)
- Expecting growth rate of AI investment to more than double
- View AI as unavoidable competitive necessity
- "Doubling down" on AI despite challenges

**Skeptics of the implementation:**
- Acutely aware of the **95% pilot failure rate**
- Know about "disconnected technology" plaguing 50% of organizations
- Face the "AI trust gap" - employees and boards don't trust the approach
- Demanding "governance at the core" and measurable ROI
- Frustrated by "pilot purgatory" and lack of enterprise-level EBIT impact

**Why this creates tension:**
- Can't stop investing (competitive pressure)
- Can't scale what they've built (organizational barriers)
- Must champion publicly while managing skepticism internally
- Need board support but board is interrogating with distrust

**The strategic posture this creates:**
- Defensive AI thesis (protect margins first, innovate second)
- Reject hype experiments, demand certain ROI
- Require CEO-led governance (can't delegate)
- Focus on foundational capabilities (data, people) before more pilots
- Shift from tech procurement to organizational change management

**Quote captures the mood:** "We are not here to talk about 'the art of the possible.' We are here to talk about execution."
