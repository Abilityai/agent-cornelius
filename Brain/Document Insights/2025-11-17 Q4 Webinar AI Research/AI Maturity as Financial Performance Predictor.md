## Core Insight

AI maturity directly predicts financial performance above or below industry average: organizations in maturity Stages 3-5 (expansion and beyond) perform above their industry's financial average, while Stages 1-2 (preparation and development) perform below average - making AI maturity not just an operational metric but a leading indicator of competitive position.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The correlation (MIT CISR 2024 research):**
- **Stages 1-2** (Preparing, Developing): 59% of organizations → Below industry average financial performance
- **Stages 3-5** (Expanding, Future-Ready, Transforming): 30% of organizations → Above industry average financial performance

**What this means:**
Being stuck in experimentation (Stages 1-2) doesn't just mean you're not capturing AI value - it means you're actively falling behind competitors who have reached expansion stage (Stage 3+). The maturity cliff represents a competitive chasm.

**High performer specifics (6% achieving 5%+ EBIT impact):**
- Pushing for transformative innovation (not incremental optimization)
- 75% have scaled or are scaling AI (vs. 33% of other organizations)
- Focus on large-scale opportunities rather than pilot proliferation
- Managing AI like transformation, not technology project
- Result: Measurable EBIT impact that shows in financial performance

**The 3x scaling advantage:**
Organizations with enterprise-wide AI strategy (characteristic of Stage 3+) are 3x more likely to successfully scale AI compared to those doing departmental experiments (Stages 1-2).

**Why the financial impact matters strategically:**
This isn't just "AI projects work better at higher maturity" (obvious) - it's that AI maturity has become a structural competitive advantage. Organizations that crack the maturity code are pulling away from industry peers financially, creating a widening gap.

**The concerning implication:**
- 59% of organizations stuck in below-average-performing Stages 1-2
- Only 30% achieving above-average performance through Stage 3+ maturity
- As AI becomes table stakes, being in bottom 59% means falling behind across all competitive dimensions
- Time to reach Stage 3+ matters - window for competitive advantage is narrowing

**What separates Stage 3+ organizations:**
Not better technology or more pilots, but systematic addressing of the three strategic gaps: unified vision/strategy, AI-ready data infrastructure, and organizational adoption capabilities.
