## Core Insight

The baseline cost of poor data quality is $12.8-12.9 million annually per organization before autonomous agents. Agentic AI transforms this "cost" into a "catastrophe." In traditional ML, the cost stems from human inefficiency (wasted time cleaning data, flawed campaigns). In GenAI, it becomes direct but limited (one chatbot hallucination = one $600 mistake). In the agentic world, give that chatbot write-access and autonomy, and one error becomes 10,000 fraudulent actions per second. The "data debt" that was previously a manageable operational cost becomes an "existential risk" the instant an autonomous agent is given the keys.

## Context & Evidence

**Three Stages of Data Quality Cost Evolution:**

1. **Traditional ML Era:** $12.9M annual cost from human inefficiency
   - Data scientists waste time cleaning data
   - Marketing campaigns based on bad data
   - Executive decisions on flawed reports
   - Impact: Slow, cumulative, human-mediated

2. **GenAI Era:** Direct but bounded errors
   - Example: Airline chatbot hallucinates refund policy
   - Airline forced to honor one $600 refund
   - Impact: Single, costly, but contained error

3. **Agentic Era:** Exponential autonomous catastrophe
   - Same chatbot with write-access to reservation & financial systems
   - Processes 10,000 requests/second
   - Issues $6 million/second in fraudulent autonomous refunds
   - Impact: Cascading, autonomous, business-ending

**The Transformation:** Data debt was a drag on velocity. Now it's a loaded gun in an autonomous system's hands.

**Quote:** "Data debt" that was previously a manageable operational cost becomes an "existential risk" the instant an autonomous agent is given the keys.
