## Core Insight

A small cohort of "AI high performers" (6-20% of companies) are achieving attributable EBIT impact of 5%+ and 22.6% productivity gains, while 80% remain stuck in pilot purgatory. This widening gap is creating a "two-tier AI economy" where the performance chasm may soon become insurmountable, making AI adoption urgent not from FOMO but from existential competitive threat.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The performance divide:**
- **6-20%** of companies are "AI high performers"
- High performers achieve:
  - **5%+ EBIT impact** attributable to AI
  - **22.6% productivity gains**
  - **3x more likely** to have redesigned workflows
  - Nearly **half** of large companies ($5B+ revenue) reaching scaling phase
- The other **80-94%**:
  - Stuck in "pilot purgatory"
  - No enterprise-level EBIT impact
  - Only use-case-level benefits at best

**What separates winners from losers:**
1. **Workflow redesign** (3x more likely in high performers)
2. **70% focus on people/culture** vs. 70% on technology (10-20-70 principle)
3. **CEO-led governance** and cultural transformation
4. **Vertical AI** integration vs. horizontal copilot deployment
5. **Large scale operations** (capital, data, and scale advantages)

**Why this creates urgency:**
- Gap is widening, not narrowing
- High performers reinvesting gains to pull further ahead
- May reach point where catching up becomes impossible
- Not FOMO - actual competitive extinction threat

**The triggers making AI urgent:**
- **Trigger 1**: Fear of being permanently left behind in two-tier economy
- **Trigger 2**: CEO's personal realization that AI is organizational (70% people), not technical (30% tech)
- **Trigger 3**: CEO decides to personally champion (BCG: "AI only delivers when CEO leads")

**Large company advantage:**
- Companies $5B+ revenue: **nearly 50%** reaching scaling phase
- Companies <$100M revenue: only **29%** reaching scaling phase
- Driven by capital to absorb failures, proprietary data, and scale ROI benefits
