## Core Insight

Companies successfully deploy "horizontal" AI copilots (general productivity tools) but these deliver "diffuse, hard-to-measure gains" that never impact the bottom line. Meanwhile, 90% of "vertical" agents (process-specific transformations) remain stuck in pilot mode. This creates the "GenAI Paradox" - success at the wrong thing, failure at the valuable thing.

---

## Context & Evidence

**McKinsey's "GenAI Paradox" Finding**:
Organizations are succeeding at deploying general-purpose copilots but failing to capture real business value because these tools spread thin gains across all employees rather than creating concentrated transformation in specific workflows.

**The Mathematics of Diffuse Impact**:
- "Improving everyone's productivity by 5%" = unmeasurable, un-bankable "gain" that vanishes into administrative slack
- "Automating 80% of debt collection workflow" (atmira model) = real, bankable gain that drops to bottom line

**Why Horizontal Wins Deployment but Loses Value**:
- Easier to deploy: No workflow redesign, minimal integration, low governance complexity
- Harder to measure: Individual productivity gains are subjective, attribution is impossible
- Never reaches P&L: Small percentage improvements across all employees don't compound to measurable business outcomes

**Why Vertical Fails Deployment but Would Win Value**:
- Harder to deploy: Requires deep integration, workflow redesign, governance frameworks
- Easier to measure: Direct financial metrics (cost reduction %, recovery rate increase, ROI)
- Directly impacts P&L: Concentrated automation in specific process shows up on income statement

**The Strategic Trap**:
Companies celebrate horizontal deployment success while remaining stuck in pilot purgatory on vertical use cases that would actually generate ROI.
