## Core Insight

Klarna cut nearly 25% of its workforce in 2024 "anticipating an AI-led future," particularly in customer service, expecting AI to replace human roles 1:1. By 2025, the company was forced to reverse course and launch a recruitment drive to rehire staff. The lesson: "The human touch remains irreplaceable in many interactions." Klarna overestimated AI's capabilities and failed to design a hybrid workflow, jumping from "Old Process" to "AI-Only Process" and discovering the catastrophic cost of that miscalculation.

---

## Context & Evidence

**What Happened**:
- 2024: Klarna cuts ~25% of workforce
- Justification: "AI-led future" will replace humans
- Target: Customer service roles primarily
- Expectation: AI can do these jobs
- 2025: Forced to rehire staff

**The Failure Pattern**:
1. **Overestimation**: Assumed AI capability = human capability in customer service
2. **1:1 Replacement Thinking**: Tried to replace humans with AI directly (no workflow redesign)
3. **Discovery**: "Human touch remains irreplaceable"
4. **Reversal**: Emergency recruitment drive (public admission of failure)

**Why It Failed**:
- **No workflow redesign**: Jumped from human-only → AI-only
- **Missed hybrid model**: Should have been human + AI, not human OR AI
- **Underestimated complexity**: Customer service requires empathy, context, judgment
- **Overestimated AI**: Hallucination, inability to understand emotional subtext

**The Three-Stage Framework (Correct Approach)**:
1. **Old Process**: Human-centric customer service
2. **AI-Assisted**: Humans use AI tools to respond faster/better
3. **AI-Centric**: REDESIGNED workflow with AI + human hybrid

**Klarna's Mistake**: Jumped from Stage 1 → Stage 3 without Stage 2 testing

**The Costs**:
- **Financial**: Severance costs + recruiting costs + training costs
- **Operational**: Service degradation during transition
- **Reputational**: Public reversal shows poor planning
- **Employee morale**: Trust destroyed with remaining staff
- **Market signal**: Cautionary tale for other companies
