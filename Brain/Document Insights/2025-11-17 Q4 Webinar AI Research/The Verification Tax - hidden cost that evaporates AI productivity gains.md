## Core Insight

The "Verification Tax" is the hidden, unaccounted-for cost that causes "promised productivity gains to often evaporate." Because AI models are "confidently wrong" and produce plausible-but-flawed outputs, human employees must spend extra time and cognitive load double-checking all AI-generated work. This work is harder than original review because confidence doesn't correlate with correctness.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The Problem**:
- AI generates outputs that "look plausible but are flawed"
- "Confidently wrong" - confidence level doesn't signal accuracy
- Humans can't quickly distinguish good from bad output
- Must spend extra cognitive effort validating everything

**Where It Hits**:
- Review queues (software development: +91% review time)
- Professional services (Deloitte Australia: $440K failed report with fabricated citations)
- Customer service (Klarna: forced to rehire staff after cutting 25% of workforce)
- Any domain requiring accuracy and accountability

**The Cost Structure**:
- **Visible Cost**: AI subscription/deployment fees
- **Hidden Cost**: Verification Tax
  - Extra human time for validation
  - Higher cognitive load (harder work)
  - Risk management and controls
  - Error correction and remediation
  - Reputational damage when verification fails

**The Deloitte Case (2025)**:
- A$440,000 government contract
- AI-generated report with fabricated citations, fake court quotes, non-existent academic works
- Root cause: "Over-reliance on AI outputs without rigorous human verification"
- Result: Contract repayment, massive reputational damage
- Classification: "Leadership failure," "control failure," not technology failure

**Why It Evaporates Gains**:
- Traditional work: Create → Review (normal cognitive load)
- AI-assisted work: AI creates (fast) → Verify (high cognitive load) → Review
- Added verification step consumes the time savings
- Verification is HARDER than creation in some cases (must spot subtle errors in plausible text)
