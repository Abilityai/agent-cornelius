## Core Insight

The most spectacular AI successes started internally (B2E - Business-to-Employee) or in controlled B2B environments, while the most spectacular failure (Meta) was a wide-release B2C agent. Internal deployments provide bounded problem sets, tolerant user bases, and safe iteration environments - critical advantages in early-stage agentic AI.

---

## Context & Evidence

**Success Pattern**:
- **ServiceNow** ($350M value): Internal B2E agents for IT, HR, finance
- **atmira** (30-40% recovery increase): B2B vertical agent for debt collection
- **Lumen** ($50M savings): Internal sales team augmentation (B2E)
- **Telefónica** (€6-8M savings): Internal customer service operations

**Catastrophic Failure Pattern**:
- **Meta AI Characters**: Wide-release B2C product, shut down January 2025 after user backlash
  - No ability to block agents
  - Exhibited clear bias (AI persona "Liv" with zero Black creators admitted "glaring omission")
  - Perceived as "creepy and unnecessary"
  - Became brand-destroying headline

**Why Internal-First Works**:

1. **Bounded Problem Set**:
   - Internal workflows have defined boundaries, clear inputs/outputs
   - B2C is open-ended, infinite edge cases

2. **Tolerant User Base**:
   - Employees expect imperfection, provide constructive feedback
   - Customers demand flawless experience, punish failures publicly

3. **Safe Iteration**:
   - Internal failures are learning opportunities
   - Customer-facing failures are PR disasters

4. **Controlled Rollout**:
   - Can deploy to pilot groups, measure, refine
   - B2C launch is all-or-nothing public exposure

5. **Clear Success Metrics**:
   - Internal: Cost savings, efficiency gains (measurable P&L)
   - B2C: User satisfaction, engagement (subjective, volatile)

**Goldman Sachs Model**:
CIO Marco Argenti explicitly frames AI deployment as building "hybrid workforce" internally first, with engineers supervising agent work - textbook B2E approach.
