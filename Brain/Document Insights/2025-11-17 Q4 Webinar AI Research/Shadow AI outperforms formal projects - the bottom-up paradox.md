## Core Insight

The MIT 2025 report found that "shadow AI"—employees using their own personal AI tools for work tasks—often delivers a BETTER ROI than formal, organization-sponsored AI initiatives. This reveals a profound organizational failure: bottom-up, grassroots AI adoption (uncontrolled, unsanctioned) is more effective than top-down, strategic projects costing $5M-$20M each.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The Finding**:
- MIT research: 95% of formal enterprise AI projects deliver zero P&L return
- Same research: Shadow AI (personal tools, no formal approval) → better ROI
- Contradiction: Informal, individual tools > Formal, funded enterprise projects

**What Is Shadow AI**:
- Employees using ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot on personal accounts
- No formal procurement or approval
- No enterprise controls or governance
- Often against IT policy

**Why Shadow AI Works**:
1. **Clear use case**: Employee knows their exact problem
2. **Immediate value**: Direct productivity boost to their work
3. **No bureaucracy**: No committees, approvals, or strategic alignment needed
4. **Fast iteration**: Try, fail, adjust in real-time
5. **User-driven**: Person using it = person benefiting from it

**Why Formal Projects Fail** (By Contrast):
1. **Vision Vacuum**: No clear strategic direction
2. **Use Case Trap**: Disconnected from real work
3. **Long deployment cycles**: 60% rejected before pilot phase
4. **Committee-driven**: Divorced from actual users
5. **No clear value**: Can't measure because wasn't defined

**The Paradox**:
- Organizations spend $30-40B on AI (MIT data)
- Employees spend $20/month on ChatGPT Plus
- The $20/month delivers more value than the millions
- Why? Because strategy is broken, not technology
