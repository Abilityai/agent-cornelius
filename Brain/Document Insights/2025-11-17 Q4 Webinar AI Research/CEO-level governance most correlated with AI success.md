## Core Insight

CEO-level oversight of AI governance is the SINGLE ELEMENT most correlated with achieving higher self-reported bottom-line impact from GenAI (McKinsey March 2025). This is not about micromanagement—it's about strategic priority signaling. When AI governance sits at the CEO level, it fills the "Vision Vacuum," prevents the "Use Case Trap," and creates the organizational alignment necessary for transformation.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The Finding**:
- McKinsey March 2025: CEO-level AI governance oversight = highest correlation with P&L impact
- Not CIO-level, not CDO-level, not committee-level
- CEO-level specifically

**Why CEO Level Matters**:
1. **Strategic Priority Signal**: CEO attention = organizational priority
2. **Resource Allocation**: CEO can reallocate budget/people across silos
3. **Cross-Functional Authority**: CEO can mandate workflow redesign across departments
4. **Vision Setting**: CEO provides "North Star" that fills Vision Vacuum
5. **Decision Speed**: CEO can cut through committee paralysis

**The High-Performer Model**:
- 6% of "High Performers" (5%+ EBIT from AI) have:
  - CEO-level governance oversight (McKinsey)
  - Dedicated AI leaders (91%, Gartner)
  - Centralized AI strategy (60%, Gartner)
- They're 3x more likely to push for transformative change (not just efficiency)

**The Failure Model**:
- 95% majority: No clear governance → Vision Vacuum
- "Leaders not steering fast enough" (McKinsey)
- Bottom-up PoC approval without strategic filter
- Result: Use Case Trap, $5M-$20M wasted per abandoned PoC

**What CEO-Level Governance Prevents**:
1. **Vision Vacuum**: CEO articulates what AI means for business model
2. **Use Case Trap**: CEO provides strategic filter for project approval
3. **Siloed Efforts**: CEO breaks down departmental boundaries
4. **Resource Competition**: CEO allocates resources to transformation, not just efficiency
5. **Impatient Abandonment**: CEO sets realistic timeline and milestones

**The Counterintuitive Element**:
- Not about CEO doing AI strategy personally
- About CEO treating AI as STRATEGIC imperative, not IT project
- Delegates execution but owns accountability
