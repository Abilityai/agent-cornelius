## Core Insight

Mid-market companies operating in the $50K-$250K project budget tier have a structural advantage over enterprise mega-spenders. Budget constraints force them to select specific, high-value workflows - which is the #1 success factor - while enterprises waste $50M+ on unfocused "science projects."

---

## Context & Evidence

**The Paradox:**
- Average enterprise spend: $1.9M on GenAI (Gartner 2024)
- 68% of enterprise leaders investing $50M-$250M (KPMG)
- 20%+ of digital budgets going to AI for high performers (McKinsey)

Yet:
- <30% of CEOs happy with investment return
- $30-40B in total investment yielding 95% zero ROI
- KPMG finding: None of the big spenders believed they had reached the stage of actually measuring ROI

**The Sweet Spot:**
$50K-$250K tier:
- Forces focus on single, high-impact project (not "boil the ocean")
- Targets specific process (RAG knowledge system, sales automation)
- Responsible for the 6-12 month paybacks and 200%+ ROIs seen in high performers
- Constraint = forced prioritization = success factor #1

**The ROI Tiers:**
- <$50K: Individual productivity tools → High adoption (88%) but zero P&L impact
- $50K-$250K: Workflow transformation → 200%+ ROI in 6-12 months (sweet spot)
- $250K+: Enterprise scale → Either massive 10x returns OR (far more likely) part of 95% failure cohort

**The Principle:**
"A focused $50K-$250K investment, applied correctly to a single workflow, will outperform a $50M enterprise 'science project' that is disconnected from the business."
