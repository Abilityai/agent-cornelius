## Core Insight

The 2024-2025 period is defined by a stark polarization: "The Great AI Divide." On one side, a small 6% minority of "High Performers" are achieving 5%+ EBIT impact and rapid ROI. On the other, 95% of organizations are getting ZERO measurable P&L return despite $30-40 billion in investment. This is not a normal distribution of outcomes—it's a bimodal split. The divide is NOT technology-driven; it's organizational maturity-driven.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The 95% (Zero P&L Return)**:
- MIT Report July 2025: 95% of orgs getting zero P&L return from GenAI
- Despite $30-40B in enterprise investment
- 60% reject enterprise tools BEFORE pilot phase
- Only 5% of integrated pilots reach production
- Operating in "Vision Vacuum" and "Use Case Trap"

**The 6% (High Performers)**:
- McKinsey: 6% of orgs attribute 5%+ of EBIT to AI
- Google Cloud: 74% report ROI within first year
- 84% go from idea to production in <6 months
- 3x more likely to pursue transformative change (not just efficiency)
- Have CEO-level governance, workflow redesign, metrics mandates

**The Apparent Contradiction**:
- MIT says 95% failure
- Google says 74% success
- How can both be true?

**The Reconciliation**:
- **Different success definitions**:
  - MIT: Measured P&L-level transformation (narrow, rigorous)
  - Google: Perceived tactical ROI (broad, inclusive)
- **Two different sides of divide**:
  - MIT measured transformative impact (95% failing)
  - Google measured tactical value (74% succeeding)

**The Key Insight**:
- Technology IS providing tactical value (individual productivity)
- 95% are FAILING to convert tactical → strategic transformation
- The gap between sides is organizational capability, not technical capability

**What Separates the 6% from 95%**:
1. **Governance**: CEO-level vs none
2. **Strategy**: Transformation vs efficiency
3. **Workflow**: Redesigned vs inserted
4. **Metrics**: Mandatory vs absent
5. **Trust**: 57% vs 14%
6. **Timeline**: 3+ years vs abandoned in <1 year

**The Bimodal Nature**:
- This is NOT normal distribution (some succeed more, some less)
- This IS bimodal (two distinct populations with different behaviors)
- Missing middle: Very few in 50% success range
- You're either building the system (6%) or stuck in trap (95%)
