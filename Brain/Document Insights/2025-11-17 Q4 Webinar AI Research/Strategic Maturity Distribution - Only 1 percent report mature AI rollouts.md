## Core Insight

Despite massive AI investment and hype, only 1% of C-suite executives describe their gen AI rollouts as mature, with 28% still in Stage 1 (Preparing/Experimenting) and only 7% reaching Stage 4 (AI Future-Ready). Critically, companies in Stages 3-4 perform above industry average financially while Stages 1-2 perform below - making AI maturity a competitive differentiator, not just operational efficiency.

---

## Context & Evidence

**AI Maturity Distribution (MIT CISR 2024):**
- Stage 1 (Preparing & Experimenting): 28%
- Stage 2 (Developing): 31%
- Stage 3 (Expanding): 22%
- Stage 4 (AI Future-Ready): 7%
- Stage 5 (Mature/Transforming): 1%

**Financial performance correlation:**
- Stages 1-2 (59% of organizations): Below industry average financial performance
- Stages 3-5 (30% of organizations): Above industry average financial performance

**The maturity cliff insight:**
Getting from experimentation (Stage 1-2) to expansion (Stage 3+) represents a fundamental shift from tactical pilots to strategic deployment - and this shift correlates directly with financial outperformance.

**What separates the 1% from everyone else:**
Not more pilots, not better technology, but:
1. Enterprise-wide strategy (not departmental experiments)
2. Focus on 3-5 strategic use cases (not dozens of pilots)
3. Centralized steering and governance
4. Dedicated budgets and executive sponsorship
5. Rigorous value tracking and iteration

**The concerning trend:**
- 75% of executives name AI as top-three strategic priority for 2025
- Yet only 39% of organizations have a strategy for AI use (down from previous years due to GenAI disruption)
- Gap between priority and strategic execution remains wide and may be growing

**High performer characteristics (6% of organizations with 5%+ EBIT impact from AI):**
- Pushing for transformative innovation (not tactical optimization)
- Three-quarters have scaled or are scaling AI (vs. one-third of other orgs)
- Focus on large-scale opportunities with clear business cases
- Manage AI like a transformation, not a technology project
