## Core Insight

The statement "security is kind of an afterthought" during AI innovation races reveals a deeper cultural value hierarchy: velocity beats safety when competing for market capture. This isn't ignorance or negligence - it's a rational prioritization pattern where speed-to-market rewards exceed security risk costs in the near term.

**Why this matters**:

1. **Velocity pressure**: First-mover advantage in AI tools (market capture)

In the current AI race, velocity consistently wins the prioritization battle.

## Unique Angle

**What makes this distinctive**:

Most analysis frames "security as afterthought" as:
- ❌ Developer negligence
- ❌ Technical oversight
- ❌ Lack of security awareness

Eugene's lens reveals it as:
- ✅ **Rational cultural value prioritization** in competitive environments

- ✅ **Systemic pattern across innovation cycles**: Not AI-specific, seen in social media, mobile apps, IoT

**The psychological mechanism**:

Security is:
- **Abstract** (potential future harm)
- **Uncertain** (may never manifest)
- **Invisible** (no dopamine reward for preventing unseen attacks)

Market capture is:
- **Concrete** (revenue, users, valuation)
- **Certain** (immediate feedback)
- **Visible** (dopamine reward from growth metrics)

Result: Security gets systematically deprioritized despite stated importance.
