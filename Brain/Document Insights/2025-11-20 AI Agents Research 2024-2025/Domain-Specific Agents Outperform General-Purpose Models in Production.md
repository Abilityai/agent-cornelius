## Core Insight

**Purpose-built, domain-specific AI agents** consistently outperform foundational general-purpose models across multiple evaluation dimensions (cost, latency, accuracy, stability, security), suggesting the future favors **specialized intelligence over general-purpose AGI**. This parallels biological evolution's preference for specialized organisms over generalists.

---

## Context & Evidence

**CLASSic Enterprise Evaluation Framework**:
1. **Cost**: Total cost of ownership (development, deployment, maintenance)
2. **Latency**: Response time and throughput
3. **Accuracy**: Correctness and reliability of outputs
4. **Stability**: Consistency and robustness across scenarios
5. **Security**: Data protection and compliance

**Key Finding**: Domain-specific agents outperform foundational models across **all five dimensions**.

**Why Specialization Wins**:

**Cost**:
- Smaller specialized models cheaper to run
- Focused training reduces compute requirements
- Efficient inference through domain constraints

**Latency**:
- Specialized models faster (smaller, optimized)
- Domain knowledge reduces reasoning steps
- Direct solutions vs. general exploration

**Accuracy**:
- Training on domain data improves precision
- Domain constraints reduce hallucination space
- Expert knowledge encoded in architecture

**Stability**:
- Narrow scope = fewer edge cases
- Domain-specific testing more thorough
- Predictable failure modes

**Security**:
- Smaller attack surface (limited capabilities)
- Domain compliance requirements built-in
- Specialized monitoring easier

## Context & Evidence (Continued)

**Examples of Successful Domain Specialization**:

**Chemistry Agents**:
- **ChemCrow**: GPT-4 + 18 domain-specific tools (synthesis, drug discovery)
- **Coscientist**: GPT-4 + robotic automation for chemistry
- Outperform general models on chemical reasoning tasks

**Biology Agents**:
- **CellAgent**: 92% task completion in transcriptomics analysis
- **ProtAgents**: Protein structure optimization via RL
- General models fail on specialized biology workflows

**Software Engineering**:
- SWE-bench Pro shows domain-specific architectures (coding agents) outperform general chat models
- Domain knowledge about repositories, dependencies, testing critical

**Customer Service**:
- Klarna's domain-specific agent: 67% chat resolution
- Generic chatbots: ~30-40% resolution
- Domain knowledge about products, policies, common issues essential

**Trajectory Prediction**:
- Future: Many specialized intelligences, not single AGI
- Specialization through training, architecture, tool access, memory organization
- Market will reward narrow expertise over broad capability
