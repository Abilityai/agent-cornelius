## Extracted Insights

### Mental Models (7 insights)

#### Insight 1: [[Impossible goals create clarity through elimination]]
- **Source**: Frame concept, lines 21-42
- **Core Insight**: An impossible goal simplifies everything by becoming a filter for what matters. Bold goals narrow options to only viable paths, while small goals leave everything seeming possible, creating paralysis.
- **Uniqueness**: Explains psychological mechanism of why constraint drives clarity - inverts conventional "keep options open" wisdom
- **Connections**: [[Finding a confirmation of belief creates dopamine spike]], [[Belief is a way to deal with Uncertainty]], [[Dopamine]]
- **Keywords**: #scaling #goal-setting #decision-making #clarity #constraint

#### Insight 2: [[Scaling requires subtraction not addition]]
- **Source**: Focus concept and supporting principles, lines 73-105
- **Core Insight**: Growth comes from subtraction, not addition. Subtraction is a higher order concept than addition - you must eliminate before you can properly focus. More variables create randomness; fewer variables enable prediction and control.
- **Uniqueness**: Inverts conventional scaling wisdom; positions elimination as prerequisite to focus
- **Connections**: [[Dopamine]], [[Flow is a selfless state]], [[In Buddhism - Self is an Illusion]]
- **Keywords**: #scaling #simplicity #focus #subtraction #essentialism

#### Insight 3: [[The Floor - dividing line between good and great]]
- **Source**: Floor concept, lines 45-70
- **Core Insight**: The "floor" is the dividing line between good and great - your standard for what you refuse to accept. Raising your floor is the crux of scaling and upward mobility. It means saying "no" to what you formerly said "yes" to.
- **Uniqueness**: Operationalizes "standards" as elimination mechanism with clear business application
- **Connections**: [[Finding a confirmation of belief creates dopamine spike]], [[Identity is a set of Beliefs]], [[Belief is a way to deal with Uncertainty]]
- **Keywords**: #scaling #standards #elimination #decision-making #floor

#### Insight 4: [[Identity-first transformation - upgrade identity before systems]]
- **Source**: Supporting principles, lines 109-116
- **Core Insight**: Your current identity produces your current results. To scale, upgrade identity before upgrading systems. Act from your future self, not present limitations. You don't scale a business - you scale a person.
- **Uniqueness**: Bridges business scaling to identity psychology; explains why systems fail without identity shift
- **Connections**: [[Identity is a set of Beliefs]], [[Finding a confirmation of belief creates dopamine spike]], [[Self is an illusion created by conceptualization]], [[In Buddhism - Self is an Illusion]]
- **Keywords**: #identity #transformation #scaling #future-self #systems

#### Insight 5: [[Time compression through constraint and focus]]
- **Source**: Time compression principle, lines 129-133
- **Core Insight**: Reframe time as distance, not duration. Compress timelines through bold decisions and focused energy. Progress stems from decision quality and learning velocity, not clock hours. Set impossible deadlines to force creative focus.
- **Uniqueness**: Time as forcing function that activates goal-based filtering; constraint creates urgency-driven clarity
- **Connections**: [[Impossible goals create clarity through elimination]], [[Dopamine]], [[Flow is a selfless state]], [[Belief is a way to deal with Uncertainty]]
- **Keywords**: #time #constraint #focus #speed #decision-making

#### Insight 6: [[10x is easier than 2x because it forces elimination]]
- **Source**: 10x vs 2x principle, lines 118-127
- **Core Insight**: 10x is easier than 2x because 10x forces focus. A 10x goal requires elimination of 80% to find the exponential 20%. 2x allows incremental optimization; 10x makes that approach mathematically impossible, forcing you to find the exponential lever.
- **Uniqueness**: Explains paradox of ambitious goals being easier through mathematical elimination requirement
- **Connections**: [[Scaling requires subtraction not addition]], [[Impossible goals create clarity through elimination]], [[The Floor - dividing line between good and great]], [[Finding a confirmation of belief creates dopamine spike]]
- **Keywords**: #scaling #10x-thinking #elimination #exponential #pareto-principle

#### Insight 7: [[Frame-Floor-Focus - the order matters]]
- **Source**: Framework overview and why order matters, lines 19-105
- **Core Insight**: The scaling framework requires sequential execution: Frame (impossible goal) → Floor (eliminate below standard) → Focus (concentrate on exponential levers). Most people skip Floor (elimination), maintaining complexity while pursuing new goals.
- **Uniqueness**: Explains why most scaling attempts fail - skipping identity death (Floor) between ambition (Frame) and execution (Focus)
- **Connections**: [[Identity-first transformation - upgrade identity before systems]], [[Scaling requires subtraction not addition]], [[10x is easier than 2x because it forces elimination]], [[The Floor - dividing line between good and great]]
- **Keywords**: #scaling #framework #sequence #elimination #methodology
