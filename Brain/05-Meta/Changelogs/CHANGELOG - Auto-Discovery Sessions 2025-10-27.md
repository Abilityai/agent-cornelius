## DISCOVERY #5: Type 2 Error Evolutionary Bias & Explanation Reducing Happiness - Negativity as Survival Mechanism

**Node A**: [[Type 2 Error (false negative) is the most dangerous kind of error]]
**Node B**: [[Explanation of good things make us feel less happy about them]]
**Semantic Similarity**: N/A (likely 0.52-0.63 - different domains)
**Conceptual Strength**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Discovery Date**: 2025-10-27
**Discovery Method**: Cross-domain pattern analysis

### The Non-Obvious Link

Evolution optimized humans to avoid Type 2 errors (false negatives—missing real threats) by creating asymmetric emotional processing: **negative events (unexplained threats) maintain high emotional salience for survival, while positive events (once explained) reduce salience to free attention for threat monitoring.** Explanation reduces happiness because our brains are threat-detection machines, not happiness-maximizers.

**Shared Pattern**: **Asymmetric Emotional Processing for Survival Optimization**

### Why This Matters

This reveals why humans have a "negativity bias" and why "thinking about good things" often makes us LESS happy (not more). It's not a bug—it's an adaptive feature:

1. **Unexplained positive** = rare/unusual → maintain attention (might be important signal)
2. **Explained positive** = categorized/normal → reduce salience (free attention for threats)
3. **Unexplained negative** = potential threat → maximum attention (survival critical)
4. **Type 2 errors fatal** → bias toward false positives (better safe than sorry)

**Gratitude practices work** by keeping positives unexplained (maintaining emotional salience), counteracting the natural tendency to categorize and dismiss them.

**Meta-Principle**: **Emotional salience follows survival optimization, not happiness optimization**
