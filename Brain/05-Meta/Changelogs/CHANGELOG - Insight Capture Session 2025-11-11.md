## Major Insights Captured

### 1. Security Theater Makes Public Agents Useless

**Core Discovery:** Public-facing agents in SaaS products must be intellectually crippled to prevent prompt injections and liability issues. Only personally controlled agents can have true autonomy because users bear the risk themselves.

**Why This Matters:**
- Explains why ChatGPT feels neutered vs local LLMs
- Reveals why enterprise AI consistently disappoints
- Predicts that transformative AI will come from individuals, not corporations

**Novel Element:** This is a decentralization argument for AI that parallels the PC revolution (mainframe → personal computer = corporate AI → personal AI)

### 2. The Agent Power Paradox

**Synthesis Achievement:** Connected the security theater insight with existing knowledge about sandboxing and autonomy to reveal a fundamental paradox: The features that make agents powerful are what make them dangerous, and the safety measures that make them safe are what make them useless.

**Pattern Recognition:** This isn't new - every transformative technology follows this pattern:
- Early Internet: Corporate intranets (safe/useless) vs open web (risky/powerful)
- Early Computing: Mainframe terminals (controlled) vs PCs (autonomous)
